Sec. 05 — Common questions

Things people actually ask.

Do we have to do Build with you first?
Most Operate clients come from a Build engagement, but no. We’ll take over an AI system someone else built if it’s in good shape. We start with a 1–2 week audit (priced separately) so we know exactly what we’re inheriting. Some audits end with “you’d be better off rebuilding,” and we say so when that’s true.
How fast can we cancel if it stops being valuable?
30 days’ notice. No penalty, no termination fee, no clawback. We hand back everything (code, runbooks, eval suites, prompts) in clean shape on the way out.
What if we want to in-house this eventually?
We’ll help. Some Operate engagements end with handoff to an in-house hire. We pair-program with your engineer, write transition docs, run knowledge sessions, and stay available for questions for 30 days after the formal end.
Can capacity scale up or down month-to-month?
Yes. Move between Light, Standard, and Heavy with 30 days’ notice. Need a temporary surge for a launch? We can add capacity for a single month. Once a year you can pause one month without losing your slot.
Do you provide an on-call SLA?
Light and Standard cover business-hours response (9–6 CT, under 2 hours on incidents). Heavy includes out-of-hours on-call with a 30-minute response SLA on Sev-1 incidents. We don’t pretend to offer 24/7 white-glove on an $8K retainer; pricing reflects coverage.
Do you take on AI systems other agencies built?
Sometimes, always after a paid 1–2 week audit so we know what we’re signing up for. If the codebase is healthy, we’ll happily take it on. If it’s not, we’ll tell you what it’d cost to rebuild and recommend who should do it, us or someone else.
